Short Bio

I am a graduate student at MIT, supervised by Prof. Daniela Rus. My research focuses on decision-making in complex dynamical systems, using methods from Game Theory, Deep Learning, and Control Theory.

I have earned the Diplôme d'Ingénieur in Applied Mathematics from the École Centrale Paris (now CentraleSupélec) and the Master of Science in Aerospace Engineering from the Georgia Institute of Technology (both completed in 2019).

Prior to joining CSAIL, I had a stint as a Control and Estimation Engineer at Parrot Drones in Paris (2020-2021). I split a gap year taken while studying at Centrale between two internships (2017-2018): first running analytics for the Louis Dreyfuss Company’s grain operations across three mills in South Africa, then at the European Space Agency's research and development center in the Netherlands, setting up safe orbit verification simulations for the tandem formation flying mission PROBA-3.
